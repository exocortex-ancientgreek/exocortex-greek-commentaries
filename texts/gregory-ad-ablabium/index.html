Commentary

3The core of this sentence is δίκαιόν ἐστι + acc + inf.
7ἀναστρέψας τὴν τάξιν: the idea is of a battle-line giving way.
9The command’s content is given by the infinitive in κατασβέννυσθαι and the following accusative object ἄνθρακας. The leading dative is instrumental.
10τοὺς ἐρημικοὺς ἄνθρακας: cf. LXX Ps 119:4 τὰ βέλη τοῦ δυνατοῦ ἠκονημένα σὺν τοῖς ἄνθραξιν τοῖς ἐρημικοῖς.

Commentary

12τύπος: ‘type’ in the sense of ‘example’, hence the dative σοί.
13ἀντιπαρέχοις: 2nd person, optative.
14διαναστήσαιμεν: ‘rouse’ in the sense of ‘call, summon’. The αι tells you this is optative.
15προέτεινας: here the idea of ‘stretch forward’ is with a question or inquiry, thus ‘propose, put forward’.
17προσηκούσης: the participle form is often used as an adjective meaning, ‘proper, fit, appropriate’.
18κατὰ… νοῦν: idiomatic for ‘at first impression’.
18συνενεχθῆναι: the form is aor.inf.pass. Take it intransitively, meaning ‘to agree with’.
20προσμαρτυρεῖν: ‘attest’ or ‘acknowledge’ might render the idea here. Notice how the verb relates to the datives and accusative that follow.

Commentary

21ἄτοπον: in a logical sense.
21παρὰ σοῦ: the usage is almost agency, but stops short of the idea that Ablabius himself makes this statement as a claim, it is something ‘said’ by him, not asserted by him.
23Καὶ οὐδὲν ἄτοπον: supply ἐστι, and then complete with the accusative + infinitive structure.
24εἶεν: 3rd pl pres opt act of εἰμί.
25συνήθεια: ‘custom’ or ‘customary usage’, a key concept for discussion of philosophy of language.
26δύο λέγειν τοὺς δύο: ‘to call two things two’, the object of the participle ἀπαγορεύων.
27ἐπὶ: ‘in the matter of’, ‘concerning’.
28ὁμολογοῦντες… ἐννοοῦντες: the participles are concessive in force.

43τῇ αὐτῇ δεικνυμένους ἀπαριθμήσαντες πληθυντικῶς ὀνομάζομεν,
44τόσους λέγοντες τοὺς ἀνθρώπους, καὶ οὐχὶ ἕνα τοὺς πάντας, ἐπὶ δὲ
45τῆς θείας φύσεως ἐκβάλλει τὸ πλῆθος τῶν θεῶν ὁ τοῦ δόγματος
46λόγος, καὶ ἀριθμῶν τὰς ὑποστάσεις, καὶ τὴν πληθυντικὴν σημασίαν
47οὐ προσδεχόμενος; Ἔστι μὲν οὖν κατὰ τὸ πρόχειρον, τοῦτο τοῖς
48ἁπλουστέροις εἰπόντα, δόξαι τι λέγειν, ὅτι φεύγων ὁ λόγος τῆς
49Ἑλληνικῆς πολυθεΐας τὴν ὁμοιότητα, θεοὺς ἐν πλήθει ἀριθμεῖν
50παρῃτήσατο, ὡς ἂν μή τις καὶ τῶν δογμάτων νομισθείη κοινότης,

Commentary

42δήποτε: with a question particle, “what then?” or “what now”; further strengthened by τοίνυν.
42τῇ καθ' ἡμᾶς συνηθείᾳ: the κατά phrase here could be rendered ‘among us’. καθ' ἕνα whereas this κατά phrase is distributive, counting ‘one by one’.
44τόσους: ‘so many’, the object of λέγοντες, i.e. we say that they are “X number of people”.
44ἐπὶ δὲ notice the δέ here: what follows contrasts with the whole sentence so far.
46τὴν πληθυντικὴν σημασίαν: ‘plural signification’, i.e. that there is a plurality of gods, even though the clause before says we number the hypostaseis. The whole sentence is a question and the leading τί is “why?”
47τὸ πρόχειρον note the substantive article, ‘what is common, ordinary’.
48φεύγων: the object is τὴν ὁμοιότητα, and the genitive phrase then depends on the object.
49πλήθει: while more commonly ‘a multitude’, ‘plural’ is often a better understanding in the context of this text.
50παρῃτήσατο: ‘begs off’, ‘declines to, refuses to’. τις agrees with κοινότης.

Commentary

381This involves two Genitive Adverbial Constructions linked by the second καί in line 381. The second one, μὴ ἀπειργούσης takes a further genitive of separation, τῆς... σχέσεως, negated by the μή. φυσικῆς… σχέσεως is a little tricky, since it is ‘natural relation’, but nature in the sense of ‘essence’ as has been used throughout the treatise.
384ἀποδοίη λόγον: The verb is optative. λόγος here means an account, and together the phrase is ‘would give an account’, i.e. a rational or dictionary definition. The two genitives then depend on λόγον.
385κατὰ τὸ πὼς εἶναι: ‘according to the how of being’, or ‘acc. to the manner of being’.
386τὸ μὲν … εἶναι: the whole first phrase is the object of εἰπόντες. The sense of the first phrase to this point is concessive. κατὰ is almost extraneous, you can render τῷ ... λόγῳ without it, but you virtually must supply the word αἴτιον either from the Critical text or else as implied.
388Πρότερον idiomatically, “firstly…” (or similar) as in English academic writing.
389πῶς ἐστι τὸ πεπιστευμένον: ‘the how of the thing believed [to exist]’. ὁ τοῦ τί ἐστι supply λόγος from l.391. ‘the account of “what” exists’, or similar.
389εἶναί πιστεύειν τι: the object of πιστεύειν is εἶναί…τι. This is clearer in the CE since τι is shifted left.

Commentary

391The point of this argument is that answering a question about the manner of existence does not answer the question of the ‘what’ of existence. To know whether the tree was planted or grew spontaneously does not resolve ‘what’ a tree is.
391τὸ οὖν ἀγεννήτως εἶναί τι λέγειν: τὸ λέγειν is the nominative subject phrase, and ἀγεννήτως ... τι is the object of that verb.
392ὑποτίθεται: here ‘to put down/forth a proposition/idea/statement’.
394αὐτομάτως: in reference to the tree, ‘had grown forth of its own’ or something similar.

400λέγοντες: ‘acknowledging’ or ‘declaring’.
