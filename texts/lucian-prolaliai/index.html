1ΠΡΟΣ ΤΟΝ ΕΙΠΟΝΤΑ, ΠΡΟΜΗΘΕΥΣ ΕΙ ΕΝ ΛΟΓΟΙΣ
2Οὐκοῦν Προμηθέα με εἶναι φής; εἰ μὲν κατὰ τοῦτο, ὦ ἄριστε,
3ὡς πηλίνων κἀμοὶ τῶν ἔργων ὄντων, γνωρίζω τὴν εἰκόνα καί φημι ὅμοιος εἶναι αὐτῷ,
4οὐδ’ ἀναίνομαι πηλοπλάθος ἀκούειν, εἰ καὶ φαυλότερος ἐμοὶ ὁ πηλὸς οἷος ἐκ τριόδου,
5βόρβορός τις παρὰ μικρόν. εἰ δὲ ὑπερεπαινῶν τοὺς λόγους

Commentary

2Οὐκοῦν ... φής a rhetorical question directed to an interlocutor who has just called the author a “Prometheus in words,” as the title indicates, “and so do you say?”
2εἶναι pres. inf. in ind. st. after φής, “say that I am”
2εἰ μὲν κατὰ τοῦτο “if by this (you mean to say)” the verb of the protasis is omitted
3ὡς ... ὄντων pres. part. in gen. abs. in ind. st. with ὡς indicating an alleged motive, “(if you mean) that my works are”
3πηλίνων gen. pred. after ὄντων, “my works are clay” because Prometheus made humans from clay in one account
3κἀμοὶ (=καὶ ἐμοὶ): dat. of possession, “also my works”
3ὅμοιος nom. pred. after εἶναι agreeing with its nominative subject. The nominative occurs in ind. st. with an infinitive when its subject is the same as the subject of the main clause, “I say that I am similar”
4ἀκούειν pres. inf. complementing ἀναίνομαι, “I don’t object to hear” i.e. to be called
4οἷος ἐκ τριόδου “such as from a place where three roads meet” i.e. a place where “trivial” things are spoken of
5παρὰ μικρόν “within a little of being slime” i.e. almost slime
5ὑπερεπαινῶν pres. part. instrumental, “if by over-praising”

Commentary

6ὡς δῆθεν with the participle ὄντας in ind. st. indicating an alleged motive (ὡς) that is not thought to be true (δῆθεν), “(implying) that my words are”
6τὸν σοφώτατον a predicate accusative after ἐπιφημίζεις, “if you apply the name the wisest of the Titans” i.e. the name Prometheus
6ἐπιφημίζεις “if you apply the name on them (i.e. the words)”
7μή ... φῇ pres. subj. in noun clause after ὅρα, “watch out lest someone say”
7οἷον agreeing with μυκτῆρα and making it indefinite, “some sort of Attic sneering”
7προσεῖναι pres. inf. of πρόσ-εἰμι in ind. st. after φῇ, “say that sneering is present to” + dat.
8τοὐμόν (=τὸ ἐμόν): “whence this ingenuity of mine”
9ἱκανὸν (sc. ἐστι): an apodosis, “(it is) enough for me if”
9μὴ πάνυ ... μηδὲ κομιδῇ note the understatement, “not completely ... and not altogether”
9γήϊνα ... ἄξια neut. pl. agreeing with the implied subject of ἔδοξεν, “if (these writings) seemed not earthen nor worthy of”
9τοῦ Καυκάσου gen. after ἄξια, the Caucasus mountains were the site of the punishment of Prometheus
10πόσῳ dat. of degree with δικαιότερον, “more justly by how much”

Commentary

11ἂν εἰκάζοισθε pres. pass. opt. pot., “you would be likened to” + dat.
11ξὺν ἀληθείᾳ a rare use of the older form of σύν, itself rarely used in later Greek except in composition, “doing contests with truth” i.e. “for real” as explained in the next clause
12ζῶα ... ἔμψυχα pred. nom. agreeing with τὰ ἔργα, “your works are living and endowed with breath”
13διάπυρον referring to Prometheus’ role in stealing fire for mortals
13ἂν εἴη pres. opt. pot., “this would be”
14πλὴν εἰ μὴ “except if not” i.e. except for one thing; this redundant expression is condemned by Lucian himself in Soloecista 7.
14ἑνὶ dat. of degree, “except for one thing (which) you might modify”
14διαλλάττοιτε pres. opt. in future less vivid protasis, “except it may differ”
14ὅτι μὴ ... πλάττετε appositional clause explaining ἑνὶ, “one thing, namely, that you do not fashion”
15ὑμῖν dat. of possession, “your fictions”
15τοῖς πολλοῖς dat., “in many cases”
15Ἡμεῖς δὲ “but we” this nominative is the subject of the relative clause below (ἄττα ἐπιδεικνύμεθα), not the main clause
15παριόντες pres. part. attributive, “we who come before ... and proclaim”
