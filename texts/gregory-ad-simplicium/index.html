Commentary

1Cor 12:11
6δῆλόν ἐστιν: the construction is idiomatic and common, ‘it’s clear’.
6προσκυνεῖν: in Patristic writing, almost always with the meaning ‘worship’.
7ἀϊδίου: the adjective is regularly used as a substantive, ‘eternity’.
8ὁ...πιστεύω: follow the article through until you get the nominative, in this case the participle. You then have an object clause of πιστεύω, with μὴ, an accusative object, an infinitive, and some modifying prepositional phrases.
10πάντως: used adverbially, πάντως is often emphatic: ‘at all events’, ‘by all means’, ‘altogether’.

Commentary

11Οὐκ...πρόσφατος. See LXX Ps 80:10 (81:9 MT)
12ποτὲ οὐκ ἦν: an Arian phrase explicitly condemned. If one follows the Paris edition, οὗτος is resumptive from ὁ λέγων. But the GNO is preferable, supplying the subject of ἦν.
14Οὐδὲποτε …ἀλλοτρίῳ: cf. Ex 20:3.
14ὁ δὲ ἀλλότριος: with ἀλλότριος supply θεός.
15The dative is one of means. πρὸς in this context supplies the factor ‘in relation to’ or ‘in respect to’.
17Notice how the two conditions work: the first is εἰ + indicative, for Gregory agrees with this protasis; the second is ἐὰν μὴ ᾖ, with the ἄν + subjunctive, because Gregory does not agree with the second protasis; the apodosis commences at the start of line 20.
17ὁ … ἔχων: ἔχω + adverb usually indicates a condition or state of being, here it is existing ‘differently’, and the genitive τῆς...φύσεως provides the different from.

Commentary

21A reference to John 10:5.
22κατασκευάσει: whether you take it with the σ (future) or ζ (present), the sense here is ‘render’, rather than ‘prepare’; see LSJ 5.
24αὐτὸν: the textual variant replaces the αὐτὸν, and makes stronger and clearer the fact that they would be worshipping a God that is a creature.
24ἰουδαΐζουσιν: it is very common to identify heretical beliefs by reference to older, typological ‘heresies’. In this case, treating the Christ as (merely) human is seen as the quintessence of a ‘Jewish’ position.
25εἰ δὲ προσκυνοῦσιν, εἰδωλολατροῦσι: i.e., on their own view they would be worshipping one who is not God, because he is created. This is explained in the following sentence.
26καὶ… καὶ: a ‘both…and…’ construction in which the infintives function as nominative gerunds.
30κατακριθῶμεν: middle/passive.
